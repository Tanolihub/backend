Main Cause of High Blood Pressure (According to
Unani Medicine)
In Unani medicine, high blood pressure is called “Zaght al-Dam”. This disease is
considered the result of Imtila-e-Dam (excess of blood), Ghalba-e-Dam
(dominance of blood), and Fasad-e-Khoon (impurity or corruption of blood).
Main causes:
Excess of blood in the body (Imtila-e-Dam)
Dominance of sanguine temperament (Ghalba-e-Dam)
Dominance of yellow bile (heat and excitation of blood)
Blood becoming thick and hot
Liver producing impure blood
Kidneys failing to remove excess water and salt
Nervous stress (grief, worry, anger)
Excessive eating, luxury lifestyle, and lack of physical activity

Symptoms in Unani medicine:

What is Diabetes (Ziya Betis)? – According
to Unani Concept
Diabetes (Ziya Betis) is essentially described as a condition of hepatic (liver)
depletion/degeneration, in which there is stimulation in the nerves and
relaxation in the muscles.
Causes of Diabetes
There can be several contributing factors:
Not getting proper sleep at night, which first affects the digestive system
Excessive use of foods that stimulate the nervous system
Psychological or mental shocks such as fear, trauma, or stress
Irregular eating habits; consuming food and drinks without hunger or thirst
Obesity: Excess fat accumulation puts pressure on the liver
Too much rest or excessive physical exertion — both disturb bodily balance
and lead toward diabetes
Vital Organs and Their Interconnection
Nature has placed three vital organs in the body:
Heart: Center of blood circulation and vital force
Brain: Center of sensations and nervous system
Liver: Center of metabolism and conversion of food into energy

Principle of Health and Disease
As long as the heart, brain, and liver remain in balance, health is maintained
If balance is disturbed in any one of them, disease appears
The entire body does not become sick at once; rather, disease begins from
the dysfunction of a single organ
Nature of Blood
Blood is essentially a compound substance
When blood passes through different organs, each organ absorbs its required
nutrients:
Nerves absorb salty nutrients
Muscles absorb acidic/sharp nutrients
Glands absorb saline substances
👉 It should be kept in mind that:
The body is nourished by food, not medicine
Medicine only produces a temporary stimulation in its target area and is then
expelled from the body
Therefore, in disease conditions, dietary control (parhez) should always be
prioritized.
